The Queen

She begged him not to go. She tried reaching for his hand, his leg, anything. She lay at his feet exhausted from crying. "But I love you, please don't leave me," she muttered softly. "Yea, right," he said emotionless.

He quickly pulled his foot away from her, stepped over her seemingly lifeless body and walked away. She lay there long after the door closed abandoned. She felt as if she'd die. Her heart felt as if someone or something was closing tighter and tighter around it. She began to wretch and cry once more but quickly bit her lip. The bite was so hard as she tried to contain her emotions, she started to bleed. She wiped it away, watching her pale hand turn bright red. The love is pouring out of me,she thought to herself. It's gone now. She laid on the floor whispering to herself, he'd never grow up and now I will never love again. No one will ever hurt me again.

The crown never rested on her head as she still didn't realize who she was. Confused she stumbled into the library. She opened both doors and gasped as she laid eyes upon them. Books, thousands of them.

They were all glistening, awaiting her touch and directly across the room hung a beautiful, intricately decorated mirror. It seemed the mirror yearned for her to look into it, to gaze at herself. She took a step into the library and spun herself around looking at the countless books that surrounded her. She faced the mirror again wondering if she dare to look at her reflection.
